### On data control

The internet's first act was to move information anywhere. Its second act, the one we're living through now, is different: for the first time, that information is being read, interpreted, and acted on by something other than a human, an agent that doesn't sleep, doesn't ask permission, and doesn't share a person's judgment about what's sensitive. The shift is large enough that even the [Vatican](https://www.vatican.va) noticed it, issuing its first doctrinal note on artificial intelligence in January 2025, [Antiqua et Nova](https://www.vatican.va/roman_curia/congregations/cfaith/documents/rc_ddf_doc_20250128_antiqua-et-nova_en.html), observing that tasks once reserved for people are now routinely handed to machines.

Every earlier layer of the internet needed its own place to enforce trust: firewalls for the network, access control for the database. Agents now move data across steps nobody is standing in front of. I think the next layer has to watch from inside that motion, not around it.
